Applicable Indian Standards

  • IS 694 — PVC Insulated Cables (up to 1100 V)
  • IS 1554 — PVC Sheathed Heavy Duty Cables
  • IS 7098 — XLPE Insulated Cables (Parts 1–3)
  • NBC 2016 Part 8 — Electrical Installations
  • IE Rules 1956 (Amended 2010)
  • IS 11171 — Short Circuit Rating of Cables
  • IEC 60364 (reference)

Voltage Drop Limits (Typical)

  • LT supply up to 1 kV — 5% max (IE Rules)
  • Motor circuits — 3% at full load
  • Motor starting — up to 15% permissible (momentary)
  • Lighting circuits — 3% max (NBC 2016)
  • Sensitive / electronic loads — 2–3%
  • Sub-mains from panel — 1.5% typical
  • DC systems (24 V / 48 V) — 2–3%
  • Long rural feeders — consult utility / DISCOMs

Selection Criteria (Priority Order)

  • 1. Current carrying capacity (Iz ≥ Ib / derating)
  • 2. Voltage drop within permissible limit
  • 3. Short circuit rating (check fault level)
  • 4. Mechanical protection requirements
  • 5. Starting current / inrush (for motors)
  • 6. Future load growth (add 20–25% buffer)
  • 7. Economics — cost vs. life-cycle losses
  • Always select the larger size if criteria conflict

Material Properties (IS)

  • Copper resistivity: 0.0172 Ω·mm²/m @ 20°C
  • Aluminium resistivity: 0.0280 Ω·mm²/m @ 20°C
  • Copper temp correction to 70°C: ×1.26
  • Copper temp correction to 90°C: ×1.38
  • Aluminium temp correction to 70°C: ×1.26
  • DC: multiply resistance by 2 (both conductors)
  • AC 1-Phase: multiply resistance by 2
  • AC 3-Phase: multiply resistance by √3 ≈ 1.732
Key Formulas Used
Design Current
Ib (AC 1Ph) = P / (V × PF)
Ib (AC 3Ph) = P / (√3 × V × PF)
Ib (DC) = P / V

Derating
Total Derating = Ct (temp) × Cg (grouping)
Required Iz = Ib / Total Derating

Cable Resistance
R/m = ρ₂₀ × Ktemp / A [Ω/m, where A is mm²]

Voltage Drop
VD (1Ph / DC) = 2 × Ib × R/m × L (V)
VD (3Ph) = √3 × Ib × R/m × L (V)
VD% = VD / V × 100
