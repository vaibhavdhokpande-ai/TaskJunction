Understanding ADC & DAC Parameters
Essential concepts for converter selection
🔢
Resolution (n bits)
The number of discrete output levels = 2ⁿ. More bits means finer granularity. Doubling bits roughly quadruples the number of levels.
📏
LSB (Least Significant Bit)
The smallest voltage step a converter can resolve. LSB = V_ref / 2ⁿ for unipolar. Smaller LSB = higher precision in measurement or output.
📶
SNR & Dynamic Range
Ideal SNR = 6.02n + 1.76 dB. Each additional bit adds ~6 dB SNR. Dynamic range = 20·log₁₀(2ⁿ) dB, representing the ratio of largest to smallest measurable signal.
🎯
ENOB (Effective Number of Bits)
Accounts for real-world noise and distortion. ENOB = (SINAD − 1.76) / 6.02. An 12-bit ADC might have ENOB of 10.5 due to noise floor.
⚡
Unipolar vs Bipolar
Unipolar: 0 to V_ref (all positive). Bipolar: –V_ref/2 to +V_ref/2 (positive and negative). Bipolar effectively halves the LSB step for the same bit count.
🔬
Typical Applications
8-bit: PWM, motor control. 10-12 bit: Arduino, general sensors. 16-bit: audio, precision instruments. 24-bit: industrial weighing, precision measurement.
